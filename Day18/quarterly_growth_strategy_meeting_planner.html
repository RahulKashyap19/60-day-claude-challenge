📝 Summary

The leadership team reviewed Q2 performance, which showed 12% revenue growth against an 18% annual target. Five enterprise deals slipped due to procurement delays and security reviews. Marketing reported improved lead generation but low conversion. Engineering capacity concerns were raised around a planned website redesign and the August analytics dashboard release. Customer support ticket volume has increased — driven by reporting performance and dashboard loading issues, which is also surfacing during sales conversations. Hiring decisions have been deferred to Q3 forecasting. The annual customer conference budget is pending vendor proposals.

💡 Key Takeaways

Q2 revenue grew 12% — 6 percentage points below the 18% committed target. Five large enterprise contracts slipped to next quarter due to procurement delays and security review requests.
Reporting performance and dashboard loading issues are the top driver of support ticket increases and are also mentioned during active sales calls — escalated as a business priority.
Engineering is at near-capacity. Adding major website work may require additional contractors. Cost estimate pending architecture review.
Website traffic is up 34% QoQ, but conversion rates remain below expectations. A website redesign and simplified demo-request process are planned.
No public commitments should be made about the August analytics dashboard release until engineering completes its milestone review.
Hiring decisions deferred until Q3 forecasts are finalized. Annual customer conference budget is pending vendor proposals — to be revisited next month.
