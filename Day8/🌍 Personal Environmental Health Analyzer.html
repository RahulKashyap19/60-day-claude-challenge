🫁 Air Quality — Body Impact

🟡 Moderate
Lung Health
Moderate PM2.5 exposure can irritate airways over time. Asthmatics and children face elevated risk at AQI 162.
🔴 High
Sleep Quality
High PM2.5 at night (AQI 162) significantly disrupts sleep cycles — increases nighttime cortisol, causes micro-awakenings, and is linked to 25% higher insomnia rates in high-pollution zones.
🔴 High
Energy Levels
Chronic exposure to AQI 162 reduces blood oxygen efficiency. Expect fatigue, mental fog, and reduced stamina. Studies show 18% lower work productivity in high-AQI environments.
🔴 High
Exercise Performance
Outdoor exercise at AQI 162 is counterproductive — you inhale 3–5× more pollutants during cardio. Shift to indoor workouts immediately.
🔴 High
Long-term Health
Living in AQI 162 is equivalent to smoking 7.4 cigarettes/day (Berkeley Earth model). 10+ year exposure raises cardiovascular disease risk by 30–40% and reduces life expectancy by 2–5 years.
🔴 High
Cardiovascular Risk
PM2.5 at these levels enters bloodstream and triggers systemic inflammation, atherosclerosis, and increased blood pressure. High-risk for heart disease over time at AQI 162.

💧 Water Quality — Skin & Hair Impact

🔴 High
Hair Fall Risk
TDS 780 ppm — very hard water. Calcium/magnesium deposits clog follicles, causing 40–60% increase in hair fall. Shampoo lathers poorly, leaving residue that weakens hair shafts.
🔴 High
Hair Dryness
High mineral content at TDS 780 forms an insoluble film on each hair strand, preventing moisture absorption. Results in brittle, frizzy, and dull hair.
🔴 High
Scalp Health
Hard water at TDS 780 disrupts scalp pH balance (ideal 4.5–5.5), promoting dandruff, excess sebum, and itchiness. Calcium deposits in follicles can cause scalp inflammation.
🔴 High
Skin Dryness
TDS 780 ppm strips skin's natural lipid barrier. Calcium ions bind to soap creating scum that dries skin. Expect tightness, flaking, and eczema flare-ups, especially in winter.
🔴 High
Acne Risk
Hard water's high pH at TDS 780 clogs pores with mineral residue, increases sebum oxidation, and disrupts skin microbiome — all contributing to acne formation, particularly jawline and cheek breakouts.
🔴 High
Sensitive Skin
Highly reactive skin will struggle most at TDS 780. Magnesium ions trigger histamine response, worsening rosacea, eczema, and contact dermatitis. Shower filter essential.

💡 Personalized Recommendations — Ghaziabad

🌅 Daily Actions

Check AQI every morning at aqi.in before planning outdoor activities
Wear N95/KN95 mask when outdoors — surgical masks block only 60% of PM2.5
Plan outdoor exercise only before 7 AM or after 8 PM when AQI dips
Avoid morning rush-hour commute windows (8–10 AM) — AQI spikes 30–40%
Consume antioxidant-rich foods (turmeric, ginger, leafy greens) to combat oxidative stress

🏠 Indoor Air Improvements

Install HEPA air purifier (CADR ≥300 m³/hr) in bedroom — priority #1 for Ghaziabad residents
Keep windows closed 9 AM–7 PM; cross-ventilate only at night
Add indoor plants: Peace Lily, Snake Plant, Spider Plant (effective at filtering VOCs)
Replace AC filters every 2 months vs standard 6 months given local AQI levels
Use beeswax candles instead of paraffin — releases zero soot particles

🏃 Outdoor Activity Guidance

AQI 150+ (current): Avoid all strenuous outdoor exercise — high cardiovascular risk
Switch to indoor gym / yoga during peak pollution months (Oct–Feb)
Post-rain days (12–24 hrs after rainfall) offer best outdoor exercise windows
Children and elderly should avoid all outdoor activity on days with AQI > 100
Consider weekend trips to cleaner NCR zones (Rishikesh, Haridwar) for fresh-air resets

💆 Hair Care Recommendations

Use clarifying shampoo once a week to remove PM2.5 deposits from scalp and hair follicles
Install shower filter rated for TDS 500–900 ppm — dramatically reduces hair fall from hard water
Apply coconut or argan oil weekly as scalp barrier against pollution particle absorption
Switch to sulphate-free shampoo — high TDS + sulphates dramatically increase scalp dryness
Rinse hair with filtered or bottled water as final step to remove hard water mineral buildup

🧴 Skin Care Recommendations

Double-cleanse face each evening: oil cleanser first (removes PM2.5), then foam cleanser
Apply niacinamide serum (4–10%) to strengthen skin barrier against PM2.5 penetration
Use SPF 50+ daily even indoors — high-AQI air amplifies UV damage by 15–20%
Install a showerhead filter to reduce chlorine and dissolved minerals that cause skin dryness
Use fragrance-free ceramide moisturizer — Ghaziabad's hard water strips natural skin oils

💧 Water Quality Solutions

Install multi-stage RO system targeting TDS output of 150–300 ppm for drinking water
Add mineralizer post-RO to restore Ca/Mg — zero-TDS water can cause mineral deficiency
Test water every 6 months from a NABL-certified lab (cost: ~₹500–1,500)
For bathing: whole-home softener or at minimum shower head filters (TDS + chlorine)
Boil drinking water as backup during monsoon — pipeline contamination risk spikes significantly
