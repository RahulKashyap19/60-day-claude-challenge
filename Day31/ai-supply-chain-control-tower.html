You've just taken the seat as Head of Operations for a global supply chain network. For the next 3 minutes, disruptions will hit your ports, plants, warehouses and fleets in real time.

Every alert competes for your attention. Choose the right response, and you protect revenue and keep customers happy. Hesitate, ignore, or choose poorly — and the network pays for it.

As the shift goes on, disruptions arrive faster and overlap. Stay sharp, prioritize ruthlessly, and keep the network green.

Objective

Maximize your operations Score over a 3-minute shift by resolving supply chain alerts with the best available action before their timers expire.

KPIs

  • Service Level — % of orders fulfilled on time.
  • Customer Satisfaction — how happy your clients are right now.
  • Inventory Health — accuracy & availability of stock.
  • Transport Efficiency — how well fleets & freight are running.
  • Operating Cost — cumulative cost of decisions (lower is better).
  • Revenue Protected — revenue saved through good calls.

Actions

Each alert offers a few contextual actions plus Ignore and Delay Decision. Only one action is the optimal fix — others cost more, help less, or backfire. Ignoring or letting an alert expire always hurts, sometimes with a delayed second penalty.

Difficulty

Alerts spawn faster and stack up as the clock runs down. Prioritize critical (red) alerts first — they expire quickest and hit hardest.
