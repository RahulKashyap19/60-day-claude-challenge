Nutrition Sources & Methodology

Data provenance, calculation methods, and app limitations.

Data Sources
USDA FoodData Central
Primary source for nutrient composition per 100g. fdc.nal.usda.gov
ICMR-NIN Nutritive Value of Indian Foods (2017)
Indian-specific values for traditional foods like Roti, Poha, Idli, Dosa.
WHO/FAO Dietary Reference Values
Recommended Daily Allowances (RDA) for micronutrients.
Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (1990)
Used for Basal Metabolic Rate calculation. Most validated formula for most populations.
Calculation Methodology
Energy (TDEE)
BMR × activity factor. Adjust ±500 kcal for weight loss/gain goals.
Protein target
1.6g/kg body weight (maintenance/muscle). 1.2g/kg for sedentary. Based on ISSN Position Stand 2017.
Macro split
Fat: 25% of TDEE. Carbs: remaining calories after protein and fat are accounted for.
Micronutrient targets
Indian RDA values from ICMR (2020) adjusted by gender and health condition where applicable.
Limitations
Nutrient values are averages
Actual values vary by variety, region, ripeness, cooking method, and brand. Cooked vs raw weights differ significantly.
Bioavailability not modelled
Iron from plant sources (non-haem) absorbs 2–5× less than haem iron from meat. Calcium absorption varies with Vitamin D status.
Individual variation
Gut microbiome, genetics, medications, and health status all affect actual nutrient needs. No formula captures this.
